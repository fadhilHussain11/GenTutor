Thermodynamics Fundamentals

1. First Law of Thermodynamics

The first law states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another. In equation form: ΔU = Q - W, where ΔU is the change in internal energy, Q is the heat added to the system, and W is the work done by the system.

2. Second Law of Thermodynamics

The second law introduces the concept of entropy, stating that in any cyclic process, the entropy will either increase or remain the same. This explains why heat flows spontaneously from hotter to colder bodies.

3. Enthalpy and Heat Transfer

Enthalpy (H) is a measurement of energy in a thermodynamic system. It includes the internal energy plus the product of pressure and volume. Heat transfer occurs through conduction, convection, or radiation.
